Metronome

About this app

This is a simple metronome app, using plain Javascript, HTML, and CSS.

How to use?

On the left you have two dropdown lists, seperated by a line. This is the time signature, i. e. how many beats there are in a bar.
For example - the most common is 4 over 4 or 4/4, which means there are 4 quarter notes in a bar of time. It is counted from 1 to 4 and then repeats after 4 pulses.
Another common time signature is 3/4, commonly used in waltzes, with three beats in a bar instead of four.
This means the top number dictates how many pulses there are in a bar of time. In this case the choice ranges from 1 to 16 notes per bar.

Now, the bottom number dictates the note value, i. e. a quarter note, eighth note, or sixteenth note.
To explain what each note value incites, let's say we are playing at 120 BPM or Beats Per Minute:

  • Quarter note (4) - at this tempo, there would be 120 quarter notes per minute,
    or if we are playing in 4/4 we would count through 30 bars of time (1-2-3-4).
    Therefore, we could call quarter notes the base pulse.

  • Eighth note (8) - the eighth notes are, as the name implies, half the duration of a quarter note.
    This means that there would be 240 eighth notes per minute at a tempo of 120 BPM.
    They are usually counted as "1-and-2-and-3-and-4-and", though sometimes also from 1 to 8 in a choreography or dancing context,
    and rarely in a musical or orchestral context.

  • Sixteenth note (16) - sixteenth notes last half the duration of an eighth note, or a quarter of the duration of a quarter note.
    So there would be 480 sixteenth notes per minute at a tempo of 120 BPM. BPM is also commonly refferred to as "quarter note equals [insert tempo]"
    Usually they are counted as "1-e-and-a-2-e-and-a-3-e-and-a-4-e-and-a", and almost never from 1 to 16.

When you select your desired time signature, a bar of notes will pop up.
This bar will have the exact number of notes, and the exact note values that you selected.
By default the first note of the bar will be pre-selected to make a distinctive sound that differs from the others.

  • This is called an "accent". It can be understood as an exclamation mark to signify a specific point in the bar.
    To make a certain note accented, simply click on it, and it will be underlined to that it is accented. Click on it again to remove the accent.
